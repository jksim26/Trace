# TRACE — Client-Facing Presentation
Combined deck: alignment summary, full 10-slide version, and the trimmed 5–7 minute elevator version.
Audience: AEC firm leadership (architects/engineers). Zero technical jargon throughout.

---

## Alignment Check — What TRACE Is, In Two Sentences

**English:** TRACE solves a problem every construction project has but nobody has fixed: the *reasoning* behind design decisions — why a choice was made, and what it depended on being true — gets lost in meetings, emails, and people's memories, so months or years later nobody can prove what was known, when, or whether a later decision quietly undermined an earlier commitment. TRACE sits quietly in the background of normal project work, automatically keeps a permanent, provable record of every decision and its reasoning, catches the moment a new decision contradicts an old one, and gives the people who carry legal responsibility for the project a paper trail they can stand behind.

**中文：** TRACE 解决的是每个建筑工程项目都有、但没人真正解决过的问题——设计决策背后"为什么这么做"的理由，以及这个决定当初依赖的前提条件，会散落在会议、邮件和人的记忆里，几个月甚至几年后，没人能证明当初知道什么、什么时候知道的，也不知道后来的某个决定是不是已经悄悄推翻了之前的承诺。TRACE 在项目正常工作的过程中安静地在背后运行，自动为每一个决策及其理由留下一份永久、可举证的记录，一旦新决策跟旧决策产生矛盾就会立刻发现，并且为那些要为项目承担法律责任的人，提供一份站得住脚的书面凭证。

## Slide 2 — The Problem
**Title:** The Vanishing Reasoning
**中文标题：** 消失的理由

**Visual:** A simple timeline showing a decision point, then a fog rolling in over it as time passes, obscuring the "why" label while the "what" label stays visible.
**视觉建议：** 一条简单的时间线，标出一个决策点，随着时间推移，一团"雾"逐渐盖住"为什么"的标签，而"做了什么"的标签仍然清晰可见。

**Bullets / 要点:**
- The decision gets recorded. The reasoning behind it lives in someone's memory, an email thread, or a meeting nobody reads back.
- When that person leaves, or six months pass, the reasoning is effectively gone.
- 决策本身会被记下来，但背后的理由只存在于某个人的脑子里、某封邮件里，或者一场没人回头看的会议纪要里。
- 一旦那个人离职，或者过了几个月，这个理由基本上就消失了。

**Speaker notes:** "Design decisions don't happen in a vacuum — they're made *because* of something: a cost constraint, a safety requirement, a client preference. The decision itself might get written down in a drawing or a spec. But the reasoning — the 'because' — almost never gets captured anywhere durable. It lives in someone's head. And people leave projects, memories fade, and inboxes get buried."
**演讲稿：** "设计决策不是凭空出现的——都是'因为'什么原因才做的：成本限制、安全要求、客户偏好。决定本身可能会被写进图纸或规格书里。但那个'为什么'——那个'因为'——几乎从来不会被记在什么能长久保存的地方，它只存在某个人的脑子里。而人会离开项目，记忆会模糊，邮箱也会被淹没。"

## Slide 3 — Why It's Getting Worse
**Title:** Why This Is a Bigger Problem Than Ever
**中文标题：** 为什么现在这个问题更严重了

**Visual:** Three icons side by side: a person walking out a door (staff turnover), a gavel (legal liability), a stack of papers growing taller (project complexity).

**Bullets / 要点:**
- Senior people leave — and take years of unwritten context with them.
- Regulators and insurers increasingly expect a provable record of *why* every safety-critical decision was made.
- The named professional on a project can be personally, even criminally, liable if they can't show what they knew and when.
- 资深人员离职时，会把多年积累的、从未写下来的背景经验一起带走。
- 监管机构和保险公司越来越要求，每一个涉及安全的关键决策，都要有可举证的"为什么"记录。
- 项目上具名负责的专业人士，如果无法证明自己当初知道什么、什么时候知道的，可能要承担个人甚至刑事责任。

**Speaker notes:** "This isn't just an inconvenience — the stakes have gone up. Turnover means your best people walk out the door with irreplaceable context. Regulators, especially after some very public building-safety failures, now expect firms to prove the reasoning behind safety decisions, not just show the final drawing. And in many places, it's not the company that's on the hook if something goes wrong — it's a named individual, personally and sometimes criminally liable, who needs to be able to say exactly what they knew and when they knew it."
**演讲稿：** "这不只是麻烦而已——风险已经升高了。人员流动意味着你最资深的人才离职时，会带走无可替代的背景经验。监管机构，尤其是在几起很受关注的建筑安全事故之后，现在要求企业证明安全相关决策背后的理由，而不只是拿出最终的图纸。而且在很多地方，出了问题要担责的不是公司，而是某个具名的个人，需要能准确说出自己当初知道什么、什么时候知道的，承担的还可能是个人甚至刑事责任。"

**要点：** 理由只存在某人脑子里或没人看的邮件里——那个人一走，理由就没了。项目上具名的专业人士，如果无法证明当初知道什么、何时知道，个人责任风险越来越大。

**Speaker notes:** "The decision gets written down. The reasoning doesn't. And when the stakes involve your professional license, 'I don't remember why' isn't acceptable."
**演讲稿：** "决定会被记下来，但理由不会——它只存在某人脑子里。而当风险涉及你的执业资格时，'我不记得为什么'是不能接受的答案。"
