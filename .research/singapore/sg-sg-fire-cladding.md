## SUMMARY
Singapore cladding fire-safety analog.

## KEY FINDINGS
- **[high]** Fire Code 2023 Cl.3.5.1 and Cl.3.15.13 require non-combustible external cladding above 15m; the 2017 Toh Guan Road fire (1 death) came from non-Class-0 Alubond panels; PFI covers buildings over 13m and over 20 years old, every 7 years.

## STRUCTURED
## SCDF Fire Code 2023 cladding rule (verified - SCDF)
- Cl.3.5.1: external wall within 1m of boundary OR exceeding 15m must be wholly non-combustible (SG analog of UK 18m/11m).
- Cl.3.5.4: cladding at least of limited combustibility per BS 476 Part 11; below 15m and 1m+ from boundary, timber 9mm+ or Class 0 (BS 476 Pt 6/7)/EN 13501-1 Class B.
- Cl.3.15.13: composite (ACP) outer layers non-combustible; external use needs a 1-hr fire-rated wall AND core to Class 0/EN 13501-1 Class B, OR assembly to NFPA 285.
- Cl.9.2.1b.(3): extra facade fire-separation for PG II floors above 24m. Basis: Fire Safety Act 1993.

## 2017 cladding episode (verified - SCDF)
- 4 May 2017, 30 Toh Guan Road; one death (Madam Neo Siew Eng, 54); ~6 weeks before Grenfell.
- Distributor Chip Soon Aluminium mixed Alubond FRB1 (Class 0, external) and FRB2 (Class 1, internal); non-Class-0 panels on ~40 buildings - as-built violated the certified premise. Owners removed cladding within two months. Reported H1-2019 tightening: factory checks, site burn tests, embossing (medium).

## PFI specifics (verified - BCA/SSO)
- Building Control (Amendment) Act passed 6 March 2020; effective 1 Jan 2022 (Regulations 2021; amendment S 594/2025). Over 13m and over 20 years old, every 7 years; ~30,000 buildings first cycle. Competent Person (PE/RA with Certificate in Facade Inspection) + registered Facade Inspector.

## SG demo
Marina Crest Residences, 28-storey ~95m condo (PG II). D-014: A1 non-combustible cladding citing Cl.3.5.1/3.5.4/3.15.13. D-031: swap to PE-core (polyethylene) ACP (combustible core, like Alubond FRB2 Class 1, not for external above 15m). Trace alert: D-031 falsifies D-014; precedent Toh Guan Road fire; would fail PFI; reject or re-certify.
