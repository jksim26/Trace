## SUMMARY
Singapore does NOT have a named "golden thread" and did not enact a Grenfell-style information-management reform analogous to the UK Building Safety Act 2022. There is no single mandated, attributable, immutable, lifecycle design-decision/safety-information record in Singapore law. Verdict: PARTIAL — no explicit golden thread, but an unusually strong personal-accountability regime that creates the demand for Trace. What Singapore DOES have is the Qualified Person (QP) system under the Building Control Act 1989. Every set of plans must be designed and submitted by a named QP — a registered Architect (Architects Act 1991 / Board of Architects) or registered Professional Engineer (Professional Engineers Act 1991 / PEB) holding a current practising certificate — and separate QPs personally supervise construction. Section 9 of the Building Control Act imposes a non-delegable PERSONAL duty on each QP to "take all reasonable steps and exercise due diligence," to notify the Commissioner of Building Control of contraventions, and (for the supervising QP, s 9(4)(d)-(e)) to keep/maintain prescribed records at site and submit prescribed reports. Breach is a criminal offence, on top of professional discipline (BOA/PEB) and civil liability.

The record-keeping that exists is fragmented and worksite/audit-oriented rather than a unified golden thread. Building Control Regulations 2003 reg 22 ("Site records," giving effect to s 9(4)(d)) requires the supervising QP to keep a prescribed list of documents at the premises, including reg 22(e) "a record of all the departures or deviations relating to the structural elements" — the closest existing analogue to a decision/change log, but it is structural-only, paper-era, and not designed to capture design rationale/assumptions or to actively detect when a later decision falsifies an earlier premise. Reg 18 requires deviations from approved plans to be applied for and amended plans submitted; as-built plans incorporating all deviations must be approved for the Certificate of Statutory Completion (CSC).

This is exactly where Trace's demand comes from. Because liability in Singapore is PERSONAL and runs in three parallel tracks (statutory/criminal under the Building Control Act and the Workplace Safety and Health Act; professional discipline before BOA/PEB; and civil negligence — Singapore courts allow recovery of pure economic loss for building defects, and departure from accepted codes is treated as prima facie evidence of negligence), a QP's defence in a BCA investigation, MOM/WSHA prosecution, or PEB/BOA inquiry rests on contemporaneous documentary proof that they exercised due diligence on each decision. Singapore's history reinforces this: the Nicoll Highway collapse (2004) led to prosecutions and a deliberate strengthening of the regime via the builders-licensing scheme and geotechnical controls (Building Control (Amendment) Act 2020); the post-Grenfell response went through fire safety (Fire Safety (Amendment) Act 2019, supply-chain accountability for non-compliant cladding) rather than an information register; and the newest reforms — the Land Sales Disqualification and Sales Suspension Frameworks effective 22 May 2026 — extend accountability to developers and their directors/shareholders. An attributable, timestamped, immutable decision-and-rationale audit trail that actively flags premise-falsifying decisions directly serves this personal-accountability/audit demand, even though no Singapore statute mandates a golden thread by name.

## STRUCTURED
# Singapore Building-Safety Accountability vs the UK "Golden Thread" — Research for Trace

## VERDICT (read first): Is there a SG golden thread? PARTIAL — effectively NO explicit golden thread; the demand comes from PERSONAL QP accountability.

- **No named "golden thread."** Singapore has no statute or regulation that mandates a single, attributable, immutable, lifecycle design-decision / safety-information record. The UK Building Safety Act 2022 golden thread (higher-risk buildings >=18m / 7 storeys, in force 1 Oct 2023) has **no Singapore equivalent**. [high]
- **But the accountability that makes such a record valuable is unusually strong.** Singapore runs the **Qualified Person (QP)** regime: a *named individual* is personally and criminally liable for design and for supervision of every project. The records that exist (Building Control Regulations reg 22 site records; reg 18 deviation/amended plans; as-built plans for CSC) are **fragmented, structural-only, worksite/audit-oriented and paper-era** — not a unified, rationale-capturing, premise-tracking "thread." [high]
- **Where Trace's demand comes from:** personal three-track liability (criminal + professional discipline + civil negligence) means a QP's defence rests on *contemporaneous documentary proof of due diligence per decision*. An attributable, timestamped, immutable decision-and-rationale trail that **actively flags when a new decision falsifies an earlier premise** is exactly the evidentiary and risk-management asset this regime rewards — it just isn't legally mandated by name. Pitch framing: *"Singapore doesn't call it a golden thread, but its QP regime makes every senior designer personally, criminally accountable for each decision — Trace gives them the defensible record the law assumes they can produce."*

## 1. Building Control Act 1989 & Building Control Regulations 2003 — design accountability + record-keeping

**Structure of duties.** Building Control Act 1989, Part 2, Division 4 ("Duties of developers, qualified persons, site supervisors and builders"):
- s 8 — Duties of developers
- **s 9 — Duties of qualified persons and specialists** (core provision)
- s 10 — Duties of site supervisors
- s 11 — Duties of builders

**s 9(1) — design QP duty (verbatim):** "Every qualified person who is appointed under section 8 or 11 to prepare the plans of any building works must — (a) take all reasonable steps and exercise due diligence to ensure that the building works are designed in accordance with (i) the provisions of this Act; and (ii) subject to section 6A, the requirements prescribed in the building regulations; (b) notify the Commissioner of Building Control of any contravention... of which the qualified person knows or ought reasonably to know; and (c) supply a copy of every plan... approved by the Commissioner of Building Control to [the site supervisors, the builder, and the supervising QP]." Where an **"alternative solution"** (non-standard design/material/method) is used, the QP must exercise due diligence to ensure it meets the prescribed objectives/performance requirements **or obtain a specialist's certification** — i.e. the Act already contemplates *documented justification of design departures from acceptable solutions* (a natural Trace hook). [high — verbatim]

**s 9(4) — supervising QP duty incl. record-keeping/reporting (verbatim):** the supervising QP must "(a) take all reasonable steps and exercise due diligence in supervising and inspecting the building works... in accordance with [the Act, building regulations, approved plans and conditions]; ... (c) notify the Commissioner... of any contravention... of which the qualified person knows or ought reasonably to know; **(d) keep and maintain at the premises on which building works are carried out such documents, books and records as may be prescribed in the building regulations; (e) submit to the Commissioner of Building Control at the prescribed times such reports and certificates as may be prescribed in the building regulations;** and (f) notify the Commissioner... if the building works have been suspended for a period of more than 3 months." [high — verbatim]

**Building Control Regulations 2003 (S 666/2003) — the prescribed records, Part III:**
- **reg 18 "Amendment and record plans of building works":** deviation from approved plans requires an application to the Commissioner under s 5A(1), accompanied by an amended plan "showing clearly the departures or deviations"; material structural changes require submission of structural plans + design calculations (and, for major works, the accredited checker). [high — verbatim]
- **reg 22 "Site records"** (expressly "For the purposes of section 9(4)(d) of the Act"): the supervising QP "shall keep and maintain at the premises... the following documents, books and records," including: (a) for major works, a 3-monthly schedule of structural works; (b) record of attendance of the QP and site supervisor; (c) **a site record book**; (d) a copy of the approved structural plans including all amendments; **(e) a record of all the departures or deviations relating to the structural elements of the building works** (provided by the design QP); (f) record of inspection/approval for concreting; (g) record of repairs to defective structural works; (h) site investigation report; (i)-(p) records of piles, pile load tests, material/cube/steel/pre-stressing/welding tests, etc. [high — verbatim]
- reg 23 — submission of progress reports and certificates; reg 24 — site supervisors; reg 24A — QP duties re geotechnical building works.

**Key analytical point for the pitch:** reg 22(e) — "a record of all the departures or deviations" — is the **closest existing Singapore analogue to a design-decision/change log**, but it is (i) structural-only, (ii) about *deviations from approved plans*, not the *rationale/assumptions* behind decisions, (iii) site-based and paper-era, and (iv) has **no falsification-detection** — nothing flags when a later decision invalidates the premise an earlier one relied on. That gap is Trace's wedge. [inference, grounded in verbatim reg text]

**As-built / CSC.** For the Certificate of Statutory Completion, a full set of as-built structural plans and design calculations incorporating all departures/deviations must be approved by the Commissioner of Building Control. [medium — secondary, consistent with reg 18]

**Retention period.** I could **not** find an explicit numeric retention period (e.g. "X years") for QP records in the regulation text extracted; reg 22 frames it as "keep and maintain at the premises" during construction. Secondary sources say documentation is retained "for post-completion audits and future regulatory reference" without a stated term. **Mark this open / low-confidence — do not assert a specific retention figure.** [low]

## 2. The QP regime and PERSONAL accountability

- **Who is a QP.** A QP is a **registered Architect** (Architects Act 1991; Board of Architects, BOA) or **registered Professional Engineer** (Professional Engineers Act 1991; PEB) holding a **current annual practising certificate** (PE Act s 18). Plans can only be submitted to BCA through a QP. [high]
- **Personal, non-delegable.** The s 9 duties and plan-submission rest on the *named* QP; accountability cannot be delegated to non-registered personnel. The supervising QP heads a site-supervision team but remains personally and legally accountable. [high/medium]
- **Commissioner of Building Control.** Statutory regulator under the Building Control Act; approves plans, receives deviation applications (reg 18) and prescribed reports (s 9(4)(e)), receives contravention notifications, approves as-built plans for CSC. [high]
- **Site supervision roles.** Below the supervising QP (QP(S)) sit **Resident Engineers (RE)** and **Resident Technical Officers (RTO)** plus site supervisors appointed under s 10; the QP(S) develops a **Site Supervision Plan** (secondary sources cite an ~S$7.5m project-value threshold for the more detailed plan — *unverified figure, low/medium*). If a site supervisor becomes unable to act, the QP must appoint a replacement and notify the Commissioner within 7 days. [medium]
- **Accredited Checkers (AC).** Independent checking of structural design for major building works (Building Control Act Division 6; reg 18(2)) — an additional accountability/record layer. [high]

## 3. Did Singapore respond to Grenfell? Yes — but via FIRE SAFETY, not an information register.

- Post-Grenfell (June 2017), **SCDF convened a cladding review panel**; 36 buildings were identified with potentially non-compliant composite panels but assessed "fit and safe for occupancy." [high]
- **Fire Safety (Amendment) Act 2019** (passed 5 Aug 2019; in force 14 Sep 2020) strengthened SCDF's regulatory/enforcement powers and made it an **offence for any party in the cladding/fire-safety-product supply chain** (suppliers, contractors, certification bodies, installers) to supply or install non-compliant regulated fire-safety products. Singapore also imposes a stricter test (on the flammable core, skins peeled). [high]
- **This is accountability-via-supply-chain liability, not a golden-thread information record.** Singapore deliberately chose enforcement powers over a Hackitt-style mandated information thread. [high/inference]

## 4. Recent (2020-2026) building-safety / accountability legislation

- **Building Control (Amendment) Act 2020 (No. 12 of 2020):** introduced/strengthened the **builders-licensing scheme** and **geotechnical building-works** controls (Commissioner may conduct random geotechnical checks before approving plans), arising from the **Nicoll Highway collapse (2004)** Joint Review Committee — phased commencements 2022-2025. The Nicoll Highway response prosecuted professionals/contractors (main contractor Nishimatsu and executives fined) and *strengthened* the accountability regime; the COI called for tougher penalties and stronger discipline for professionals. [high; Nishimatsu detail medium/secondary]
- **Building Control (Amendment) Act 2024:** **energy efficiency of existing buildings only** — *not* an accountability/record reform (confirmed verbatim from the Bill's long title). Do not cite it as accountability reform. [high — verbatim]
- **Land Sales Disqualification Framework + Sales Suspension Framework (effective 22 May 2026):** bans of up to **5 years** on developers who deliver unsafe or repeatedly major-defect projects ("recalcitrant behaviour"); future projects can carry no-sale licence conditions; **directors, shareholders and associated entities** can be caught (anti-restructuring). Most significant recent expansion of *developer* accountability. [high concept / medium on exact mechanics — secondary; verify against BCA primary notice]

## 5. PI / liability exposure and how a decision audit trail helps

- **Three parallel liability tracks** from one design failure: (1) **Statutory/criminal** — Building Control Act s 9 (reported design ~up to S$200k + 2 yrs; supervision ~up to S$100k + 2 yrs) and Workplace Safety and Health Act (designer's duty); (2) **Professional discipline** — PEB/BOA inquiry, suspension/cancellation of practising certificate; (3) **Civil** — contract + tort negligence, including **pure economic loss for building defects** (recoverable in Singapore). [medium — figures/structure secondary]
- **Evidentiary standard.** Departure from accepted codes of practice is treated as **prima facie evidence of negligence** (*MCST Plan 1075 v RSP Architects & Planners (Eastern Lagoon)* — verify citation), and an **incomplete or "perfunctory" supervision log is itself treated as prima facie evidence of negligence**. The burden effectively falls on the QP to show contemporaneous due diligence. [medium — secondary; the negligence-standard proposition is well established in SG construction law]
- **PI insurance.** Required for corporate/LLP practice (BOA for architectural firms; PEB for licensed corporations/LLPs); PE Act sets no statutory minimum (tenders often require ~S$10m); policies are **claims-made**. [medium — secondary]
- **Trace value proposition (grounded):** In a BCA investigation, MOM/WSHA prosecution, or PEB/BOA inquiry, the QP must prove *what they decided, why, on what assumptions, and when*. Trace supplies an **attributable, timestamped, immutable** record per decision — directly rebutting the "perfunctory log = negligence" inference — and its **premise-falsification alerts** operationalise the s 9 "ought reasonably to know" standard and the s 9 contravention-notification duty by surfacing when a new decision undermines an earlier safety-critical assumption *before* it becomes a defect. This converts a manual, fragmented, structural-only paper trail (reg 22) into a defensible, lifecycle, decision-level thread. [inference, anchored to verbatim s 9 / reg 22]
