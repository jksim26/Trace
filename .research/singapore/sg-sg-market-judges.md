## Trace - Singapore + APAC Market Pain and Judge-Facing Narrative

### 1. Singapore productivity problem (home-market spine)
- About 1 percent/year productivity growth for two decades vs about 3.6 percent manufacturing and about 2.8 percent world economy (medium). BCA tools: Construction Productivity and Capability Fund SG$250m; Productivity Innovation Project subsidising up to 70 percent of tech-adoption cost.
- Foreign-manpower dependency: about 378,000 foreign Work Permit holders in construction (largest single sector); construction-marine-process about 30 percent of total foreign workforce Dec 2023 (medium, re-confirm with MOM).
- COVID inflection point: 2020 dorms were about 88 percent of national COVID cases at the early peak (17,758 of 20,198 by 6 May 2020), driving reduce-manpower, go-digital, DfMA policy (high).
- Abortive work/rework is the enemy: CORENET X (mandatory GFA 30,000m2+ from 1 Oct 2025) cuts abortive reworks via upfront BIM de-confliction (high). No SG-specific rework percent published; global benchmarks: rework about 9 percent of cost, about 18 percent schedule slip (CII); about US$1,080 per RFI, about 9.9 RFIs per US$1M. Positioning: abortive work is what happens when an earlier design decision is silently falsified by a later one and nobody catches it - the gap Trace closes.

### 2. Built Environment ITM - quantified targets Trace supports
- DfMA: target 70 percent of GFA by 2025; about 51 percent 2022, about 61 percent 2023, about 68 percent 2024 (high).
- IDD: target 70 percent of GFA by 2025 (from 34 percent baseline) (high).
- Funding: SG$100m BETC grant from Apr 2025 (5 years); SG$250m CPCF.
- Framing: IDD links stakeholders across the lifecycle to improve decision-making and avoid abortive works. Trace is the decision-memory layer inside IDD - it remembers WHY each decision was made and alerts when a new decision breaks an old premise.

### 3. China and APAC context (make it THEIR problem)
- Scale: world's largest construction market, about US$4.8 trillion 2025, about 20 percent of global construction investment (medium).
- Top-down digitalisation: national BIM mandate (from about 2017); 14th Five-Year-Plan intelligent-construction and digital-twin city agenda (2021-2025); provincial BIM targets up to about 70 percent.
- Killer analog: China's lifelong quality responsibility system. Under PRC construction law, project leaders and the five responsible parties (owner, surveyor, designer, contractor, supervisor) bear permanent personal liability for quality across the building's service life (medium; MOHURD Order No. 5 from 2014 unconfirmed). Bridge: Trace makes lifelong accountability enforceable - an immutable, timestamped, authored trail of who decided what and why. A China judge already believes the principle; Trace operationalises it.

### 4. Recommended framing: BOTH, sequenced (do NOT lead UK-only)
1. Universal pain (30s): un-tracked decisions lead to silent premise-falsification, then abortive work, rework, safety risk. Quantify: rework about 9 percent of cost plus 18 percent delay.
2. Home market = Singapore (the demo): localise to a Singapore project; tie to BCA ITM (DfMA and IDD 70 percent by 2025) and CORENET X's abortive-work mission. Signals built for a real, regulated APAC market.
3. Scale plus relevance = China/APAC: largest market on earth plus national BIM and intelligent-construction mandate plus its own lifelong-responsibility law. The problem is already a legal duty in China; Trace makes it auditable.
4. Global seriousness = UK golden thread (one line): after Grenfell a G7 government legislated a criminally-enforced, immutable, attributable building record. Trace is that, automated, with active falsification alerts. UK = credibility multiplier, not core market.

Judge takeaway: Singapore's ITM wants 70 percent digital delivery and CORENET X exists to kill abortive work; China legally requires lifelong quality accountability; the UK criminalised losing the golden thread after people died - Trace is the memory agent that makes all three real by capturing every design decision's rationale and alerting the moment a new decision falsifies an old one.
