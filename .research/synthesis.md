# SYNTHESIS

## demoNarrative
TITLE THE DEMO: "Maple Wharf — the decision nobody remembered making, until it nearly failed Gateway 2."

Frame the project once on a title card (10 sec): Maple Wharf is a fictional 21-storey residential building. Because it is over 18m it is a Higher-Risk Building, so the UK Building Safety Act 2022 "golden thread" applies — every safety decision must be attributable to a named person, timestamped, and immutable. Say it out loud: this turns the tool from a productivity nicety into legally-required infrastructure. Then run three scenes, each a short fictional meeting transcript on screen with the agent reacting live in a side panel.

SCENE 1 — CAPTURE (RIBA Stage 2 Concept Design, 14 Jan 2026). Show ~6 transcript lines:
  Architect (RW): "On the envelope — we're 21 storeys, 64m, an HRB, so cladding has to be A2-s1,d0 or better. I propose a terracotta rainscreen on mineral wool."
  Fire engineer (PD): "Agreed. Anything combustible over 18m is non-compliant under Approved Document B. Terracotta and stone wool are A1/A2. Lock it in."
  QS (SM): "It's about £190/m² though."
  Architect: "It's a compliance requirement, not a preference. Moving on."
The agent silently writes a structured record and reads it back: "Captured D-047 — Facade = terracotta rainscreen on A2-s1,d0 mineral wool. WHY: HRB >18m; non-combustible mandated by ADB B4. WHEN: 2026-01-14 11:42. WHO: R. Wells (arch), P. Desai (fire). ASSUMES: building remains HRB; combustible cladding prohibited. STATUS: valid." Land the point: we captured the WHY and the assumptions it rests on — exactly what minutes, RFIs and BIM models drop.

SCENE 2 — ACTIVE INVALIDATION (Value Engineering workshop, RIBA Stage 4, 3 Mar 2026 — seven weeks later, different people in the room):
  Contractor (JT): "Budget's £400k over. Biggest soft target is the facade. We swap the terracotta for an equivalent aluminium composite rainscreen — visually identical, saves ~£180k."
  QS (SM): "Client will like it."
  Contractor: "It's covered under the 'or equivalent' clause, no need to reopen the design."
THE MONEY MOMENT — before anyone agrees, the agent fires an unprompted red INVALIDATION alert: "⚠ CONFLICT — Proposed VE substitution (ACM rainscreen) contradicts the live premise of D-047 (14 Jan, R. Wells / P. Desai). D-047's rationale: 'non-combustible mandated because building is an HRB >18m.' ACM is combustible — NOT an 'equivalent' under ADB B4. Accepting it would (1) breach the golden thread, (2) require Gateway 2 re-submission to the Building Safety Regulator, (3) re-open the fire strategy, cavity barriers and Part L thermal model. Downstream rework: facade support steel, fixings, fire-stopping, energy model re-run."
  Contractor: "...I didn't know that decision existed."
Narration: clash detection would only catch this when the two materials physically collide in the model — months later, as rework. We caught it the instant the premise was contradicted, in the meeting, before it became a Gateway failure. Then show the bi-temporal mechanic explicitly: the agent never deletes. If the team overrides D-047, its validity is closed (valid_to set), a new decision is linked via supersedes/superseded_by, and the full "changed by whom, when, why" chain is preserved — precisely the immutable, attributable record the BSA golden thread demands.

SCENE 3 — RECALL UNDER A TIGHT CONTEXT BUDGET (Gateway 2 dossier prep, 12 May 2026; a new graduate, AL, has just joined):
  Graduate (AL): "3,000 pages of minutes and 40 RFIs on this job. The Regulator wants us to evidence the facade decision. Why terracotta, and can we still change it?"
The agent's retrieval pulls back exactly three items into the answer — D-047 (decision + fire rationale + named authors + date), the 3 Mar attempted ACM substitution and why it was rejected, and the ADB B4 constraint it depends on — and explicitly nothing else. Show a "context budget: 1,180 / 8,000 tokens" meter to make the constraint visible. The agent answers in two cited sentences. Then, to demonstrate abstention, AL asks "did we ever decide the balustrade material?" and the agent replies "No decision on record; not yet decided" rather than hallucinating. Narration: this is the Track-1 brief stated literally — "recalling critical memories within a limited context window" — and it is also the firm's professional-liability defence, generated in seconds instead of a paralegal-week of minute-trawling.

CLOSE (15 sec): "Clash detection catches two ducts hitting a beam. We catch the moment a budget decision quietly breaks a fire-safety decision someone made seven weeks ago — before it becomes an RFI, a Gateway failure, or a tragedy." Cut to the architecture diagram. Total run ~3-4 minutes, inside either video limit.

## differentiation
No competitor closes the four-part loop of decision + rationale + timestamp + active downstream invalidation, and that gap is precise enough to defend to a sharp judge. AEC incumbents (Revizto, Newforma Konekt, BIMcollab, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Aconex, Procore) track issues, clashes and documents with timestamps and audit trails but store WHAT is wrong in the model, never WHY a choice was made or whether a past choice still holds; AEC AI startups (Helonic, Drawer AI, Togal) do forward, point-in-time error detection on a drawing set, not longitudinal decision memory. The closest analog — software ADRs — captures decision + rationale + a "superseded-by" link, but supersession is a manual human status flip, software-only, with no automatic detection and no AEC awareness. Horizontal AI memory (Supermemory, Second Brain, Mem0/Zep) and generic KB contradiction-detectors (Alhena, Fini) already handle abstract contradictions, so "we detect conflicts" is NOT itself novel — say so on stage to stay credible. The defensible wedge is the combination nobody ships: an AEC-domain decision-dependency graph (decisions as first-class nodes carrying rationale + assumptions, edges encoding "assumes/depends-on") that performs ACTIVE premise-invalidation — pushing an alert the instant a new decision falsifies the premise an earlier one relied on, rather than waiting to be searched — and is golden-thread native (attributable, timestamped, immutable), turning a memory agent into BSA-2022 compliance infrastructure for higher-risk buildings.

## architectureRecommendation
Adopt a Graphiti-style bi-temporal decision graph as the system of record, driven by Qwen, wrapped in a Letta-style in-context core block. Concretely: (1) Reasoning/extraction/contradiction engine = qwen3.7-max on Model Studio via the OpenAI-compatible Singapore endpoint (https://dashscope-intl.aliyuncs.com/compatible-mode/v1 or the per-workspace ap-southeast-1 maas domain) — 1M context, native iterative function calling; you must opt into the big window via max_input_tokens (default usable ~129K). Use qwen-plus/qwen-flash as the cheaper default for routine extraction, escalating to qwen3.7-max only for contradiction reasoning, and use qwen-flash context caching to amortize the stable brief prefix. (2) Memory store: each decision is an edge {subject, predicate, object, valid_from, valid_to, recorded_at, superseded_at, supersedes/superseded_by, importance, rationale, assumptions[], discipline, author, source_episode}. For a hackathon use a lightweight bi-temporal schema over SQLite + a vector index rather than standing up Neo4j — graph BFS over a single project's decisions is cheap and SQLite is zero-ops; cite Neo4j/DashVector as the production path. Never hard-delete: contradiction sets the old edge's valid_to and links superseded_by, preserving the immutable golden-thread trail. (3) Retrieval = contextual hybrid: text-embedding-v4 at 1024d (cost/quality sweet spot) + BM25 + qwen3-rerank (hosted Singapore limits: max 500 docs, 4,000 tokens/item, 120,000/request — NOT the 32K open-source context), then composite re-score on relevance+recency+importance, filtered to valid_to IS NULL by default with an explicit "history/audit" mode. Your existing wiki-retrieve skill already implements the contextual-prefix+BM25+rerank pattern and is a sensible primitive to reuse. (4) Working memory = a bounded always-in-context core block holding the live brief summary + currently-valid decisions + house rules. (5) Judging hook: expose capture_decision and check_invalidation as MCP tools / custom skills through the Qwen-Agent framework (QwenLM/Qwen-Agent, install extra [mcp]) — the most direct way to earn "custom skills / MCP integrations" credit. (6) Active invalidation runs on every new decision ingest (LLM compares the new edge against semantically-related existing edges, prioritizing new info). (7) Stretch: a background "sleep-time" consolidation pass and a small LongMemEval-style eval harness scoring knowledge-update, temporal, and abstention. Avoid qwen-long (10M) — it is Beijing-only and your SG free quota/coupon likely won't cover it.
